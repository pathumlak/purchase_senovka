# Project Proposal: SENOVKA ERP Operations System

## 1. Proposal Title
SENOVKA ERP: Integrated Inventory, Customer Pricing, Billing, Settlement, and Operational Reporting Platform

## 2. Executive Summary
This proposal presents a complete business operations platform that unifies inventory control, customer-specific pricing, billing, cheque settlement tracking, booking-order execution, operational alerts, and downloadable management reporting in one system.

The system is designed to replace fragmented spreadsheets and manual bookkeeping with a reliable workflow where each transaction updates stock, customer balances, and operational records consistently. It supports day-to-day execution and management-level visibility through filters, dashboards, logs, and exportable reports.

## 3. Problem Statement
Small and medium businesses often face the following operational issues:

1. Product stock is tracked manually, causing stockout surprises and data mismatch.
2. Customer-specific prices are difficult to maintain accurately.
3. Billing across cash, credit, and cheque is inconsistent and error-prone.
4. Cheque maturity and booking send-dates are missed due to no alert mechanism.
5. Teams cannot quickly generate filtered reports for decisions and audits.
6. Critical actions (create, complete, settle) are not recorded in one activity stream.

## 4. Proposed Solution
The proposed system provides one operational hub for production, customer, and billing teams. It enforces business rules at transaction time and provides filtered exports for business review.

The key value is operational continuity:

1. Every sale updates financial and stock context.
2. Every booking can be tracked and converted to an actual bill safely.
3. Every cheque settlement updates customer balances transparently.
4. Every key event is visible via dashboard notifications and logs.

## 5. What You Can Do With This System (Exact Functional Capability)

### 5.1 Inventory and Product Operations
With this system, you can:

1. Create and maintain product categories.
2. Add products with quantity, size, and descriptions.
3. Track stock quantity in real time.
4. Identify low stock, critical stock, and out-of-stock items.
5. Prevent deletion of products already used in billing history.
6. Export filtered category reports to PDF and Excel.

Business outcome:

1. Better stock planning.
2. Reduced stock-related sales disruption.

### 5.2 Customer and Pricing Control
With this system, you can:

1. Create and manage customer profiles.
2. Maintain customer balances.
3. Assign custom product prices per customer.
4. Ensure one valid price mapping per customer-product pair.
5. Block customer deletion when financial history exists.

Business outcome:

1. Accurate customer billing terms.
2. Reduced pricing disputes and pricing errors.

### 5.3 Billing Execution (Cash, Credit, Cheque)
With this system, you can:

1. Create bills with line-level products, quantities, and pricing.
2. Handle multiple payment methods (cash, credit, cheque).
3. Automatically compute totals, discount, net value, and paid amount.
4. Auto-generate invoice IDs and maintain transaction chronology.
5. Update stock and customer balance according to payment logic.
6. View invoice details and generate bill PDF/print output.
7. Filter All Bills by period, customer, payment type, status, and search.
8. Export filtered All Bills data to PDF and Excel.
9. Filter cash and cheque sales separately and export each report.

Business outcome:

1. Faster and accurate billing.
2. Reliable financial record consistency.

### 5.4 Booking Orders (Send-Later Workflow)
With this system, you can:

1. Create pending booking orders for future dispatch.
2. Track pending vs completed booking states.
3. Edit pending bookings before final conversion.
4. Complete a booking order and convert it into a final bill.
5. Auto-apply stock reduction and customer balance updates on completion.
6. Filter booking orders by status, customer, payment type, date range, and search.
7. Export filtered booking-order data to PDF and Excel.
8. Print completed linked bills directly from booking-order actions.

Business outcome:

1. Strong control over deferred orders.
2. Clear transition from booking promise to financial transaction.

### 5.5 Sold Goods and Cheque Settlement Control
With this system, you can:

1. Record cheque settlement entries with bank, branch, and maturity details.
2. Link settlement records to customer and original bill.
3. Automatically compute deducted amount, extra stored value, and post-settlement balance.
4. Monitor completed vs pending settlement records.
5. Filter sold-goods records by customer, status, maturity range, and search.
6. Export filtered settlement data to PDF and Excel.

Business outcome:

1. Strong cheque lifecycle visibility.
2. Better receivable control and follow-up planning.

### 5.6 Dashboard and Alerts
With this system, you can:

1. View operational KPIs on one homepage.
2. Track total products, customers, bills today, and monthly revenue.
3. Monitor low-stock risk indicators.
4. Receive proactive alerts for:
   1. Low-stock products
   2. Upcoming cheque maturities
   3. Pending booking send-dates
5. View recent activity feed for fast operational awareness.

Business outcome:

1. Quicker decisions and reduced missed deadlines.

### 5.7 Unified Logs and Audit-Style Visibility
With this system, you can:

1. View all major operational events in one log center.
2. Filter logs by category (billing, settlements, customers, bookings, alerts).
3. See latest activity first.
4. Paginate logs for usability.
5. Export filtered logs to PDF and Excel.

Business outcome:

1. Better traceability for management and internal reviews.

## 6. Reporting and Management Use
This system supports practical management reporting needs:

1. Daily monitoring of sales and settlements.
2. Weekly operational review of pending bookings and due cheque actions.
3. Customer-level pricing and balance follow-up.
4. Export-ready data for meetings, audits, and compliance checks.

3. Better control over pending and completed operations.

### 7.2 Financial Benefits
1. Improved receivable visibility.
2. Better cash/cheque tracking.
3. Lower risk of data inconsistency in customer balances.

### 7.3 Management Benefits
1. One-screen KPI monitoring.

## 8. Scope of Use
Primary users this system supports:

1. Billing and accounts operators.
2. Production and inventory coordinators.
3. Operations managers.
4. Business owners needing report-level visibility.

## 9. Key Deliverables of This Proposal

1. Fully functional ERP web application covering inventory, customers, and billing.
2. Filter-driven operational screens with pagination for large datasets.
3. PDF and Excel exports across critical modules.
4. Printable invoice support.
5. Dashboard alerts and unified logs for decision-making.

## 10. Success Criteria
The project is successful when:

1. All major daily transactions are executed inside the system.
2. Teams can generate filtered reports without manual spreadsheet work.
3. Stock and customer balances remain consistent after transactions.
4. Pending tasks (cheques/bookings/low stock) are visibly tracked and actionable.

## 11. Conclusion
SENOVKA ERP is a practical, operations-first platform that converts scattered manual business processes into a controlled, measurable, and report-ready workflow.

It gives the business not only transaction execution but also clarity: what happened, what is pending, what is risky, and what action should be taken next.
